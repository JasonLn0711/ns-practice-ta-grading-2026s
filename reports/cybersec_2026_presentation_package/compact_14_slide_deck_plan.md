# CYBERSEC 2026 Compact 14-Slide Deck Plan

Purpose: compress the 23-slide CYBERSEC talk into a tighter 14-slide conference deck while preserving the full scoring strategy, technical seriousness, and required organizer compliance.

This compact version is the recommended next deck-editing target. The 23-slide package remains the source of detail; this file defines the more stage-efficient delivery deck.

## 1. Compression Strategy

The compact deck keeps the five memory anchors and removes slide-change overhead:

1. `你賣的不是模型`
2. Four product scales
3. Evidence chain
4. `Cyber Safety Is Patient Safety`
5. Patch SLA and `30/60/90`

The compression is not a content deletion exercise. It is a narrative sharpening exercise: fewer slide surfaces, stronger visual objects, more time per idea, and less risk of sounding like a checklist.

| Compression Decision | What Changes | Scoring Rationale |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Keep title and disclaimer separate | Slide 1 and slide 2 remain fixed | Protects organizer compliance and opening rhythm |
| Merge trend and urgency | Original slides 4-5 become one `why now` slide | Keeps relevance high without a news/trend detour |
| Keep four-scale map as its own slide | Original slide 6 remains a standalone map | Protects Structure and Narrative Design |
| Merge FDA 524B and FDA 2025 into evidence chain | Original slides 7-9 become one regulatory evidence slide | Reduces legal density while preserving Content Depth |
| Merge TFDA, NIST, and AI stack | Original slides 10-12 become one operating-language slide | Keeps local relevance and management language without three framework slides |
| Pair product scales | Original slides 13-16 become two comparison slides | Preserves architecture logic while reducing repetition |
| Preserve patient-safety peak | Original slide 17 remains standalone | Protects Audience Impact |
| Merge white-box and black-box | Original slides 18-19 become one testing strategy slide | Improves contrast and pacing |
| Preserve Patch SLA | Original slide 20 remains standalone | Protects the operational climax |
| Merge playbook and roadmap | Original slides 21-22 become one implementation slide | Leaves the audience with a clear next action |
| Preserve close | Original slide 23 remains standalone | Protects memorability and Stage Presence |

| Separate agenda slide | The four-scale map is the agenda and is more memorable |

## 7. Scoring Risk And Mitigation

| Risk From Compression | Mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| Content may feel too compressed | Let slides 6 and 7 carry regulatory depth; use Q&A for advanced details |
| Product scale may feel rushed | Keep slide 5 as standalone map and use paired scale slides with consistent layout |
| Audience may miss testing detail | Speak output artifacts: finding list, retest evidence, Patch SLA |
| Ending may feel abrupt | Preserve slide 13 roadmap and slide 14 final principle as separate slides |
| Visual density may rise | Use two-panel layouts only for paired comparisons; keep all memory slides phrase-led |
